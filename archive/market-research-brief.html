Hackathon Market Research

Customer Intelligence Brief

Feb 2026 · 3 Problem Spaces

Research synthesized from Deloitte, Slush Startup Survey 2025, PYMNTS, and industry reports. Each problem space mapped against: target customers, key pain gaps, and opportunity applications.

01
Financial Data → Decision Making
High Technical Bar
Target Customers
FP&A Analysts & Finance Teams
Mid-market companies (50–5,000 employees) drowning in manual Excel workflows. Spend 80% of time on data prep, 20% on actual analysis.
Portfolio Managers & Investment Analysts
Hedge funds, family offices, and independent RIAs who need multi-source signal aggregation without Bloomberg-level cost ($20k+/yr).
SMB Owners & Operators
Revenue $500k–$10M. Making pricing, hiring, and inventory decisions without a finance team. Flying blind on forward-looking data.
Risk & Compliance Officers
Banks and fintechs required to aggregate risk data under BCBS 239. Data quality and timeliness are regulatory obligations, not just preferences.
Non-profit & Government Budget Officers
Constrained to free/cheap tools but dealing with complex, multi-fund accounting and grant tracking that commercial tools handle poorly.
Key Gaps & Pain Points
Data Access Latency 90%+ of bank data users say data they need is unavailable or too slow to retrieve (Deloitte 2024). Reporting cycles of 1–2 weeks are standard.
Excel Hell Persists Finance teams still live in Excel despite ERP adoption. Manual export → transform → model cycles introduce errors and kill analysis time.
Siloed Data Architecture CRM, ERP, banking, and marketing data never talk to each other. Unified financial view requires expensive enterprise tooling (SAP, Oracle).
No Accessible Scenario Planning Only 16% of FP&A teams can run scenarios in under a day. 20% can't run them at all. Most tools are either too simple or too complex.
Insight ≠ Action Dashboards show what happened. Nothing tells you what to do next. The gap between data and decision remains filled by expensive consultants.
Application Opportunities
AI Scenario Engine for SMBs
Natural language interface: "What happens to my margins if cost of goods rises 12%?" Connects to real bank data. No finance degree required.
Multi-source Signal Aggregator
Pulls market data, industry benchmarks, and internal financials into a single view with anomaly alerts. Bloomberg Lite for the 99%.
Opportunity Detector
Identifies patterns across financial data (receivables, inventory cycles, industry signals) and surfaces specific, timed decisions: "Renegotiate lease in Q2."
Live Excel Sync Layer
Bridge between ERP data and Excel — auto-populates models with live data. Kills the export-transform loop without replacing existing workflows.
02
Personal Finance Management & Access
Crowded Market
Target Customers
The Financial Anxiety Majority
60% of consumers experience financial anxiety. Only 37% use advanced budgeting tools. The 63% gap is massive — they want help but won't open another app.
Gig Workers & Irregular Income Earners
Uber drivers, freelancers, contractors, seasonal workers globally. Traditional budgeting tools assume monthly salary. Their income is lumpy, unpredictable, variable.
Financially Excluded Adults
1.4 billion adults globally unbanked. Can't access credit, insurance, or savings products. Mobile-first populations in SE Asia, Africa, Latin America.
New-to-Country Immigrants
No credit history in new country. Face systemic barriers to financial products despite economic stability in home country. Invisible to traditional credit scoring.
Post-Mint Refugees (US)
Millions displaced after Mint shut down in 2024. Still searching for a replacement with similar simplicity. High acquisition opportunity, clear need.
Key Gaps & Pain Points
One-Size-Fits-All Tools Apps built for salaried, banked, English-speaking users. Gig workers, immigrants, and low-income earners are afterthoughts, not primary design targets.
Engagement Collapse Users open budgeting apps in crisis, abandon them in 2 weeks. No mechanism to stay useful when finances feel stable. Engagement is reactive, not habitual.
Knowledge Gap, Not Tool Gap Many users don't lack a budgeting app — they lack financial literacy. They don't know what to do with the data the app shows them. Insight without context is useless.
Trust & Privacy Barrier Users reluctant to share banking credentials. CFPB finalized open banking rules Oct 2024 — still building consumer trust in data sharing. Especially acute in non-Western markets.
Credit Invisibility Billions of people have real financial behavior (rent, phone, utilities) but no formal credit score. They're locked out of financial services that could transform their lives.
Application Opportunities
Gig Income Smoother
Automatically holds and releases income to simulate stable monthly salary. Handles tax set-asides, smooths variable cash flow. Built for the irregular earner.
Alternative Credit Builder
Uses rent, subscriptions, utilities, and phone payments to build credit profiles for the unscored. Could unlock mortgages, loans, and insurance for billions.
Employer Embedded Finance
Financial wellness delivered through employers — not as an app people download, but as a benefit. Harvard research shows employees spend 8hrs/week on money stress at work.
Conversation-first Financial Coach
AI that explains what your financial data means, not just shows it. "Your subscriptions increased 40% — here's what to cut." Action, not charts.
03
Tools to Aid Startup Creation
Best Differentiation
Target Customers
First-time Founders (Pre-idea or Idea Stage)
No playbook. Don't know what they don't know. Wasting weeks on validation that could take days. Often build before confirming anyone wants it.
Solo Founders (35% of all startups, only 17% get VC)
Wearing every hat — product, marketing, legal, finance, HR. Structurally disadvantaged vs. multi-founder teams despite no evidence they perform worse.
Non-technical Founders
Great domain expertise, no developer skills. Can't scope an MVP, can't evaluate developer quotes, can't protect themselves from tech debt and agency overscoping.
Repeat Founders in New Domains
Know how to build companies, but entering unfamiliar verticals (e.g., a SaaS founder going into biotech). Need domain-specific intelligence quickly.
Accelerator & Incubator Cohort Members
Intensive programs with 90-day timelines. Need to move from concept to investor-ready in weeks. Mentorship is sparse; tools that scale mentor wisdom are high value.
Key Gaps & Pain Points
Fundraising Paradox 58% of founders cite fundraising as #1 challenge (Slush 2025). To raise, you need growth. Growth requires capital. The loop is hard to break without warm network access.
Conversion Gap in Sales 68% of founders find prospects easily. Only 30% can convert them. Finding customers is solved. Closing them — especially in B2B — is not.
Over 60% Underestimate Operational Complexity "More hats than expected" is universal. Founders burn out managing legal, HR, finance, and ops with no playbook or sequencing guide.
Validation Theater vs. Real PMF Founders build, launch, and call it "validated" based on friends saying "cool idea." No structured, fast process for getting real signal before building.
Investor Signal Blindness Founders don't know what investors actually see when they review a deck. No feedback loop until rejection, which comes with no explanation.
Application Opportunities
Idea Validation Engine
Structured framework that takes a startup concept and returns real signal: competitive landscape, demand proxies, target customer archetypes, and kill risks — in minutes.
Investor Readiness Simulator
Upload your pitch deck, get back an investor's perspective: what questions they'll ask, what they'll challenge, what's missing. Trained on real investor frameworks.
Founder Operating System
Stage-gated task sequencer for the first 12 months. Tells you what to do next based on your company stage — not a generic checklist, a dynamic decision tree.
Solo Founder Co-pilot
AI that fills the co-founder gap: challenges assumptions, plays devil's advocate, holds the founder accountable, and provides the external perspective a solo founder lacks.
